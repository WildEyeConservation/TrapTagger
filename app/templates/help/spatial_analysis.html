Spatial analysis

Spatial Analysis is a powerful tool that allows you to explore species sightings in relation to space, providing valuable insights into species distribution and habitat preferences.

Key features of Spatial Analysis include:

  • Data Unit Selection: Choose the data unit level to view species counts, allowing you to focus on clusters, images, sightings, or custom x time to independence levels.
  • Species Selection: Select the species you wish to analyze, allowing you to focus on specific species or all species in the reserve.
  • Normalisation: Optionally, normalise the heatmap by trap density across the reserve. This reduces bias in areas with more cameras, providing a more accurate view of species distribution.
  • Radius Adjustment: Modify the radius parameter to control the size of the area-averaged, helping you reveal different patterns in species distribution.
  • Map Selection: Choose from a variety of map options, including Google-owned and open-source satellite and street maps, to visualize species distribution effectively.
  • Exclude Irrelevant Data: Customize your analysis by excluding empty images, knocked-down cameras, and images containing vehicles, humans, and livestock. This allows you to focus solely on the heatmap for the animals in the reserve.
  • Relative Abundance Index: Optionally, normalise the heatmap by site effort days to calculate the Relative Abundance Index (RAI). This allows you to compare species distribution across sites with different operational periods.

Once you've configured your analysis, you can export the map as an image by clicking the Export button. Please note that only OpenStreetMaps can be exported.

Spatial Analysis empowers you to uncover spatial patterns in species distribution, making it an essential tool for habitat assessment and conservation efforts.
