Diversity Indices

Diversity indices are used to measure the diversity of species in a given area. The following diversity indices are calculated:

  • Shannon-Wiener Index:

    where i is the species, S is the total number of species, and pi is the proportion of species i in the community.

  • Simpson's Index(D):

    where i is the species, S is the total number of species, ni is the number of individuals of species i, and N is the total number of individuals in the community.

  • Simpson's Index of Diversity (1-D):

    It is calculated as 1-D, where D is Simpson's index. (See above)

  • Hill number (q=2):

    It is calculated as 1/D, where D is Simpson's index. (See above)

  • Pielou's Evenness:

    where H is the Shannon-Wiener index and S is the total number of species.

  • Species Richness:

    where i is the species, S is the total number of species, and pi is the proportion of species i in the community.

The diversity indices summary is useful for determining the diversity of species in your data. A date range can be specified to limit the data used in the analysis. The data unit specified is used to calculate the proportion of each species in the community.

Please click the Generate Results button to generate the summary results. Please note the summary results may take a while to generate if you have a large amount of data.
